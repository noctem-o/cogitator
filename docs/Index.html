COGITATOR

Can you prove what your AI agent did?

A tamper-evident audit layer for AI agents. Cryptographic proof of what your agent did, tried to do, and was blocked from doing — independently verifiable by any third party.

The Problem

Regulated AI needs more than a log file.

AI agents are already operating in financial services, healthcare, and legal workflows. When something goes wrong — a bad trade, a missed diagnostic flag, a contract clause the agent should not have touched — your organisation faces a question it currently cannot answer.

"Can you prove the agent did exactly what you say it did, and nothing it was not supposed to?"

Existing audit logs are mutable. Post-hoc summaries are reconstructions. There is no standard way for a regulator, an insurer, or a counterparty to independently verify your account of what your AI actually did.

This is not a gap most teams notice until they are sitting across from a regulator or a claims adjuster. By then, the only evidence available is the operator's own logs — an inherent conflict of interest.

What It Does

A cryptographic record that anyone can verify.

COGITATOR wraps around an AI agent and produces a cryptographic witness root — a single tamper-evident fingerprint — after every run. Any third party can recompute that fingerprint from the same inputs and confirm the record was never altered after the fact.

Before any tool call is dispatched, COGITATOR checks it against a declarative policy file. Allowed calls are executed and recorded. Blocked calls are also recorded — proving not just what the agent did, but what it tried to do and was prevented from doing.

The result is a self-contained, independently verifiable bundle: every agent step, every tool call, every policy decision, sealed cryptographically.

```
01
What it did

Every action taken, recorded in a tamper-evident chain.

02
What it tried

Every blocked call recorded, not just permitted ones.

03
What was prevented

Policy decisions committed into the audit record, before dispatch.

```
What You Get

Built for audit, procurement, and legal scrutiny.

  • Regulatory evidence A concrete artifact satisfying EU AI Act Articles 12 and 9 tamper-evident record-keeping requirements, and aligned with FCA, SEC, and FDA SaMD audit trail expectations.
  • Independent verification No need to trust operator infrastructure. Any auditor, regulator, or counterparty can rerun the verification themselves from the same inputs.
  • Pre-call control A policy layer that enforces operational boundaries before side effects happen — not after. The agent cannot reach outside its sanctioned envelope.
  • Byte-stable replay Any prior run can be re-executed from the same inputs and will produce the identical cryptographic fingerprint. Reproducibility is not a claim — it is a verifiable property.
  • Liability clarity In the event of a dispute, both parties work from the same verifiable record. Not competing log extracts. Not operator-provided summaries.

Regulatory Alignment

Designed against real regulatory requirements.

Framework Requirement addressed
EU AI Act Articles 12 and 9 — tamper-evident record-keeping and risk management systems for high-risk AI.
FCA / SEC Mandatory audit trails for high-autonomy AI systems acting in financial markets.
FDA SaMD Software as a Medical Device audit trail guidance — verifiable record of AI decision steps.
